# Key Business Insights
## Superstore Sales Analysis

---

### 1. Overall Business Performance
- Total Revenue: $2,326,154 across 5,111 orders
- Total Profit: $292,274 with 12.56% profit margin
- 804 unique customers with average 6.4 orders per customer
- Repeat order rate indicates strong customer loyalty

### 2. Yearly Growth Trend
- Revenue grew 51% from $493K (2023) to $745K (2026)
- Profit margins improved from 10.46% to 13.46% over 4 years
- Order volume grew consistently every year
- Business shows healthy and sustainable growth trajectory

### 3. Monthly Seasonality
- Q4 (Sep-Dec) consistently highest revenue months every year
- January/February consistently lowest performing months
- November 2026 recorded highest single month revenue of $118,454
- Two months showed negative profit despite positive revenue
  suggesting heavy discounting impact

---
### 4. Regional Performance
- West region leads with $739K revenue and 14.98% profit margin
- Central region is most concerning — only 7.92% margin despite $503K revenue
- South region shows growth potential being underdeveloped market

### 5. State Level Findings (Critical)
- California is single largest market — $457K revenue, $76K profit
- Texas is 3rd highest revenue state but losing -$25,729 (biggest loss)
- 5 states are loss making with combined losses of ~$78,346
- Loss making states: Texas, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Illinois, North Carolina
- Central region poor performance explained by Texas & Illinois losses

### 6. Category Performance
- Technology is star category — $146K profit at 17.45% margin
- Furniture is biggest problem — only 2.61% margin despite $754K revenue
- Office Supplies is hidden hero — 17.22% margin, highest units sold

### 7. Sub Category Findings (Critical)
- Tables is biggest loss maker — -$17,753 loss on $208K revenue (-8.53%)
- Only 3 of 17 sub categories are loss making — problem is fixable
- Copiers have highest profit ($56K) with 37.21% margin
- Paper has highest margin % at 43.39%
- Fixing Tables alone would improve total profit by 6%

### 8. Top Products
- Canon imageCLASS Copier is single most profitable product — $25K profit
- 8 of top 10 profitable products are Technology
- High value low volume products outperform high volume low value ones

### 9. Customer Segment Analysis
- Consumer segment = 50% of total revenue at $1.17M
- Corporate highest avg revenue per customer at $2,994
- All three segments profitable — healthy diversification

### 10. Individual Customer Findings
- Sean Miller — highest revenue ($25K) but losing -$1,980
- Tamara Chand — best customer with 47% profit margin
- Highest revenue customer ≠ most profitable customer

### 11. Discount Impact (Most Critical Finding)
- Orders above 20% discount consistently generate losses
- 31-50% discount range loses avg $155 per order
- $125,507 total profit destroyed by excessive discounting
- Recommendation: Cap all discounts at maximum 20%

### 12. Shipping Analysis
- Standard Class = 60% of orders, most profitable by volume
- All shipping modes are profitable
- Same Day underutilized at only 266 orders

---
## KEY BUSINESS RECOMMENDATION
The single biggest opportunity to improve profitability is
capping discounts at 20%. This alone could recover up to
$125,507 in lost profit — a 43% improvement in total profit.
